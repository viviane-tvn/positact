RESTORE BLUE

Their Intenentions

Restore Blue understands the intrinsic but giving value of coastal wetlands – saltmarsh, mangroves, mudflats, seagrass meadows – as an essential piece of the climate solution. They recognize that they provide ecosystem services, such as biodiversity enhancement, coastal protection, water quality maintenance, and adaptation benefits to coastal communities. They also have the ability to sequester and store “blue” carbon from both the atmosphere and oceans. This unique quality, combined with the others, make them one of the most productive ecosystems on Earth. However, when they are degraded or destroyed, these ecosystems instead emit centuries-old, stored carbon, turning them into sources of greenhouse gases. Thus, Restore Blue works to protect and restore coastal and marine ecosystems in the hope of mitigating impacts of the climate crisis.

Their Aims

Humans have destroyed 87% of global wetlands for mostly agricultural purposes, with roughly 35% of the world’s mangroves being destroyed in the last 20 years. Approximately 50% of saltmarsh wetlands and 52-78% mangroves have been destroyed in Australia since colonisation. Restore Blue aims “restore a minimum of 10,000 hectares of lost blue carbon wetlands by 2030” (Restore Blue, n.d.).

They collaborate with global partners – Indigenous peoples, farmers, governments, NGOs and business – to restore and conserve coastal ecosystems. These wetlands, which were found on mostly private lands, were drained when farmers were encouraged to convert them to what was considered to be more “productive” land (for agriculture). Now, these farmers must be incentivised to restore them for the health of the coast and environment. Restore Blue aims to do this through the sale of carbon: premium blue carbon credits.

Their Strategies

Restore Blue’s tools are the best-available science and rigorous legal standards (co-founded and owned by Dr. Emma Carmody, an international environmental lawyer). They use these tools to make “growing blue carbon on your property” (Restore Blue, n.d.) attractive to landholders.

Their scientific research is used to encourage landowners to contact them to start projects for environmentally conscious and sustainable reasons. The other method of making coastal wetland restoration attractive to private owners is to incentivise them financially. Using their legal expertise, they create opportunity costs. This is done through the trade of premium blue carbon credits – these are the financial tools to get restoration done. The blue carbon method was legislated in Australia on January 2022 under Carbon Credits (Carbon Farming Initiative ) Act 2011 (CFI Act). Restore Blue evaluates private land and incentivise landowners to turn farmland into marshland by showing them how to make the same or more profits per hectare through carbon credits.

So far, coastal wetland restoration has been through philanthropic and governmental funding, primarily on private land. However, in order to scale this climate solution, Restore Blue is looking for ways to restore coastal ecosystems publicly, and are doing their best to get wetlands included into the EPBCT Act, which is being re-written soon.
