PROJECT DRAWDOWN

Their Intenentions

Project Drawdown is “the world’s leading resource for climate solutions” (Project Drawdown, n.d.)! In other words, it is the world’s leading climate solutions database. It intends to, quite simply, stop climate change as quickly, safely, and equitably as possible through the collection of research-backed databases of solutions. Its reach is broad: it works across all sectors, time, and geographies to maximize co-benefits and eliminate barriers. It works on all aspects of the climate equation by focussing on stopping sources of emissions and supporting sinks, as well as supporting society in achieving broader transformations. In short, it supports all projects that have any capacity to help the world reach “drawdown” – the tipping point of greenhouse gases emissions into its decline.

    Project Drawdown pursues these actions “globally, simultaneously, and with determination:
  • Reduce Sources — bringing emissions to zero
  • Support Sinks — uplifting nature’s carbon cycle
  • Improve Society — fostering equality for all” (Project Drawdown, n.d.).

Their Aims & Strategies

Its inherent goal is this: in order for the world to reach drawdown, it must work together. The climate crisis is a global problem – the world’s largest, shared challenge – with a unanimous agreement that this existential threat affects all life on the planet. Project Drawdown facilitates this by collecting libraries of science-backed solutions across all sectors and geographies. It is by sharing information – existing scientific and technological advancements – that the collective can attain drawdown. It uses research and analysis to focus on solutions that are available, scalable, quantifiable, financially viable, and have a net positive impact. These must reduce greenhouse gases by avoiding or sequestering emissions, and are evaluated through profound analysis of scientific literature and modelling. A climate solution is only as effective as its ability to be put to effect, and the world benefits from Project Drawdown’s capacity to share these proven models. The aim is to use research to illustrate how the world can reach drawdown by 2050 using currently existing and available climate solutions.

Project Drawdown also aims to advance the next level of business leadership on climate solutions through its Drawdown Labs. It is its private sector testing group for facilitating the integration of climate solutions quickly, safely, and equitably. It recognizes the private sector’s potential – influence and capital – in maximize their contribution towards reaching “net zero”. By engaging employees in climate solutions, it contributes skills, resources, and people into the movement. It encourages businesses to set a new standard for climate leadership, and to shift capital away from carbon-intensive activities.

Its other subgroup, Drawdown Lift, aims to deepen understanding of the links between climate change solutions and poverty alleviation, especially in countries of the Global South. Many communities, mostly in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia hit the trifecta of injustice: they bear little responsibility for emissions, have low accessibility to resources, and their location leads to high exposure to climate threats. In short, the least equipped to protect themselves face the greatest risks from impacts. Drawdown Lift works to advance climate solutions that have benefits for boosting the well-being of these rural communities through the synthesis of knowledge, connection of leaders and audiences, and encouragement of decision-makers.
